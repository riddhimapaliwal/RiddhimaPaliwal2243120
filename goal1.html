SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 1: No Poverty

  • General Description

    • Goal 1 of the SDGs, "No Poverty," aims to eradicate poverty in all its forms and dimensions. Poverty is not just about lack of income; it encompasses various factors such as lack of access to education, healthcare, clean water, and basic resources that hinder human well-being and development. This goal addresses the need to ensure that all people, regardless of their background, have the opportunity to live a dignified and fulfilling life.

  • Targets

    • Eradicate Extreme Poverty : The primary target is to eliminate extreme poverty for all people everywhere. This involves lifting individuals living on less than $1.90 a day out of extreme poverty.

    • Reduce Overall Poverty : Reduce by at least half the proportion of men, women, and children of all ages living in poverty, considering multidimensional poverty measurements.

    • Equal Rights to Resources : Ensure that all men and women, in particular the poor and vulnerable, have equal rights to economic resources, including ownership and control over land and other forms of property.

    • ESocial Protection Systems : Implement nationally appropriate social protection systems and measures, including social safety nets, to ensure that all individuals have access to basic needs during times of vulnerability.

  • Progress


    • Significant progress has been made in reducing extreme poverty globally. The number of people living in extreme poverty has decreased over the years, thanks to focused efforts and economic development. However, challenges remain in reducing poverty disparities between urban and rural areas, addressing inequalities, and reaching vulnerable groups such as women, children, and marginalized communities.

    • Achieving Goal 1 requires not only addressing economic aspects but also addressing the underlying factors that perpetuate poverty, such as lack of access to quality education, healthcare, clean water, and decent work. It requires a comprehensive approach that combines social, economic, and environmental strategies to lift individuals and communities out of poverty while leaving no one behind.
