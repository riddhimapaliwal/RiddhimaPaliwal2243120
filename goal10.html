SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 10: Reduced Inequality

  • General Description

    • Goal 10 of the SDGs, "Reduced Inequality," aims to reduce inequality within and among countries. This goal recognizes that inequalities in income, wealth, and opportunities can hinder sustainable development and social cohesion. It emphasizes the importance of ensuring that economic growth benefits all segments of society, particularly those who are marginalized or vulnerable.

  • Targets

    • Income Inequalities: By 2030, progressively achieve and sustain income growth for the bottom 40% of the population at a rate higher than the national average.

    • Empowerment and Representation : Ensure equal opportunity and reduce inequalities of outcome, including through policies that enable women’s and men’s full and effective participation and equal opportunities for leadership at all levels of decision-making in political, economic, and public life.

    • Social, Economic, and Political Inclusion: Ensure equal opportunities and reduce inequalities of outcome, including through measures to eliminate discrimination, and by fostering policies that promote inclusion.

    • Remittances and Financial Services: Enhance the use of enabling technologies, in particular information and communications technology, to promote the empowerment of all people.

  • Progress


    • Efforts have led to improvements in infrastructure development, innovation, and industrialization in various regions. Investments in transportation, energy, and communication systems have improved connectivity and supported economic growth. However, challenges remain in achieving sustainable industrialization while minimizing negative environmental impacts. Bridging the digital divide and ensuring equal access to technology and innovation are also areas of concern. Additionally, the global transition to sustainable infrastructure requires investment and collaboration.

    • Achieving Goal 9 involves adopting sustainable practices in industry, fostering research and development, and investing in modern and resilient infrastructure. Balancing economic growth with environmental sustainability is key to ensuring that development benefits present and future generations.
