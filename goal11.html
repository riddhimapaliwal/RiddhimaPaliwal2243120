Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

  • General Description

    • Goal 11 of the SDGs, "Sustainable Cities and Communities," aims to make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. This goal recognizes the rapid urbanization happening globally and emphasizes the importance of creating livable and sustainable urban environments for present and future generations.

  • Targets

    • Safe and Inclusive Public Spaces: Ensure access for all to adequate, safe, and inclusive public spaces, particularly for women and children, older persons, and persons with disabilities.

    • Affordable and Sustainable Housing: Provide access to safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable housing and basic services for all.

    • Cultural and Natural Heritage: Enhance the use of enabling technologies, in particular information and communications technology, to promote the empowerment of all people.

  • Progress


    • Efforts have led to improved urban planning, increased access to basic services, and enhanced infrastructure in many cities. Urban areas are becoming more connected and efficient through sustainable transportation systems and smart city technologies. However, challenges remain in managing rapid urbanization, addressing slums and informal settlements, and providing equitable access to resources and opportunities. Ensuring the resilience of cities to natural disasters and promoting cultural heritage preservation are ongoing concerns.

    • Achieving Goal 11 requires integrated urban planning that considers social, economic, and environmental factors. It involves collaboration among governments, local communities, and stakeholders to create inclusive, sustainable, and resilient cities that offer quality living conditions for all.
