SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 14: Life Below Water

  • General Description

    • Goal 14 of the SDGs, "Life Below Water," aims to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development. This goal recognizes the critical role that oceans and marine life play in supporting life on Earth and in providing essential ecosystem services. It emphasizes the need to address the threats facing marine environments and ensure their health and vitality.

  • Targets

    • Marine Pollution: Prevent and significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds, particularly from land-based activities.

    • Sustainable Fishing: Sustainably manage and protect marine and coastal ecosystems to avoid significant adverse impacts.

    • Conservation of Ecosystems: Minimize and address the impacts of ocean acidification, including through enhanced scientific cooperation at all levels.

    • Marine Protected Areas: Increase the economic benefits to small island developing states and least developed countries from the sustainable use of marine resources.

  • Progress


    • Efforts have led to increased awareness about the importance of ocean conservation, the establishment of marine protected areas, and the reduction of plastic waste entering oceans. Sustainable fishing practices are being promoted to ensure the long-term health of marine ecosystems. However, marine pollution, overfishing, habitat degradation, and climate change continue to threaten marine biodiversity and ecosystems. Ocean acidification, caused by increasing carbon dioxide levels, also poses a significant threat to marine life.

    • Achieving Goal 14 requires international cooperation to reduce pollution, regulate fishing practices, and establish marine protected areas. Sustainable management of marine resources and the reduction of human impacts on oceans are essential to ensure the health and resilience of marine ecosystems.
