SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 15: Life on Land

  • General Description

    • Goal 15 of the SDGs, "Life on Land," aims to protect, restore, and sustainably manage terrestrial ecosystems and halt biodiversity loss. This goal recognizes the importance of healthy ecosystems for human well-being, including the provision of resources, clean air and water, and climate regulation. It emphasizes the need to conserve biodiversity and promote sustainable land use practices.

  • Targets

    • Biodiversity Conservation Take urgent and significant action to reduce the degradation of natural habitats, halt the loss of biodiversity, and protect and prevent the extinction of threatened species.

    • Sustainable Forest Management:Promote the implementation of sustainable management of all types of forests, halt deforestation, restore degraded forests, and substantially increase afforestation and reforestation.

    • Combat Desertification: Take action to combat desertification, restore degraded land, and soil, including land affected by desertification, drought, and floods.

    • Conservation of Mountain Ecosystems: Ensure the conservation, restoration, and sustainable use of terrestrial and inland freshwater ecosystems and their services, in particular forests, wetlands, mountains, and drylands.

  • Progress


    • Efforts have led to increased awareness about the importance of biodiversity conservation, the establishment of protected areas, and the restoration of degraded ecosystems. Some regions have made progress in combating desertification and promoting sustainable land management practices. However, biodiversity loss continues at an alarming rate, with habitat destruction, pollution, invasive species, and climate change as major drivers. Deforestation and land degradation pose significant threats to ecosystems and contribute to climate change.

    • Achieving Goal 15 requires conservation efforts that span ecosystems and species. It involves promoting sustainable forestry, restoring degraded land, and ensuring that human activities do not exceed the planet's capacity to support life.
