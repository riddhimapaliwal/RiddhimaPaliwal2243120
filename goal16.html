SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions

  • General Description

    • Goal 16 of the SDGs, "Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions," aims to promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, provide access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable, and inclusive institutions at all levels. This goal recognizes the importance of peace, stability, and effective governance in achieving sustainable development.

  • Targets

    • Reduce Violence and Crime: Significantly reduce all forms of violence and related death rates everywhere.

    • Access to Justice: Promote the rule of law at the national and international levels and ensure equal access to justice for all.

    • Accountable Institutions: Ensure responsive, inclusive, participatory, and representative decision-making at all levels.

    • Anti-Corruption: Substantially reduce corruption and bribery in all their forms.

  • Progress


    • Efforts have been made to reduce violent conflicts, strengthen institutions, and promote access to justice in various regions. International initiatives and agreements have aimed at promoting peace, stability, and human rights.However, challenges persist in achieving lasting peace, addressing corruption, and ensuring effective governance. Violent conflicts, human rights violations, and unequal access to justice continue to threaten stability and development in many areas.

    • Achieving Goal 16 requires strong institutions that uphold the rule of law, promote human rights, and ensure transparency and accountability. Fostering peace and justice requires addressing root causes of conflict, promoting social cohesion, and creating an enabling environment for inclusive and sustainable development.
