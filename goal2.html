SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 2: Zero Hunger

  • General Description

    • Goal 2 of the SDGs, "Zero Hunger," aims to end all forms of hunger and malnutrition by ensuring access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food for all. It recognizes that hunger is a complex issue influenced by factors such as poverty, inequality, and lack of access to resources. Achieving this goal is crucial for improving the health, well-being, and potential of individuals and communities.

  • Targets

    • End Hunger : The main target is to end hunger and ensure access by all people, in particular, the poor and people in vulnerable situations, to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food all year round.

    • Improve Nutrition : Achieve substantial improvements in nutrition by increasing the availability and consumption of nutritious foods, especially for infants, young children, pregnant women, and lactating mothers.

    • Agricultural Productivity: Double the agricultural productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers, particularly women, indigenous peoples, family farmers, and pastoralists.

    • Sustainable Food Production: Ensure sustainable food production systems and implement resilient agricultural practices that increase productivity and production while maintaining the ecosystems and biodiversity.

  • Progress


    • Significant progress has been made in reducing the number of people suffering from hunger globally. Efforts to improve agricultural practices, enhance rural infrastructure, and promote social protection programs have contributed to reducing food insecurity. However, challenges remain in addressing inequalities in food distribution, addressing malnutrition and stunting, and ensuring access to nutritious food for vulnerable populations.

    • While progress has been made, recent global challenges, such as conflicts, climate change, and the COVID-19 pandemic, have highlighted the need for more resilient and sustainable food systems. Achieving Zero Hunger requires collaboration between governments, international organizations, the private sector, and civil society to ensure that food is available, accessible, and nutritious for all, and that the root causes of hunger are addressed.
