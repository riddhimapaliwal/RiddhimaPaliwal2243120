SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



Goal 4: Quality Education

  • General Description

    • Goal 4 of the SDGs, "Quality Education," aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all. This goal recognizes education as a fundamental right and a powerful driver of personal and societal development. Achieving this goal entails providing accessible, relevant, and high-quality education for individuals of all ages.

  • Targets

    • Equal Access to Education : Ensure that all girls and boys have access to free, quality primary and secondary education, regardless of gender, ethnicity, or socioeconomic background.

    • Skills Development: Ensure that all learners acquire knowledge and skills needed to promote sustainable development, including education for sustainable development and global citizenship.

    • Gender Parity and Equality : Eliminate gender disparities in education and ensure equal access to all levels of education and vocational training for vulnerable populations.

    • Quality Education: Substantially improve the quality of education by enhancing learning environments, teacher training, and educational resources.

  • Progress


    • Progress has been made in increasing enrollment in primary education and narrowing the gender gap in education. Efforts to improve access to education, enhance infrastructure, and promote literacy have contributed to positive trends.

    • However, challenges remain in achieving universal primary and secondary education, especially in marginalized and conflict-affected areas. Quality education, teacher training, and learning outcomes are also areas of concern. The COVID-19 pandemic further highlighted disparities in digital access and learning opportunities.Achieving Goal 4 requires investment in educational infrastructure, teacher training, and curriculum development. It also involves addressing societal factors that hinder access to education, such as child labor, early marriage, and discrimination.
