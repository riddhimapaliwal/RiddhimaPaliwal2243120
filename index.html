SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS



  • What Are Suatainable Development Goal?

    • Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), also known as Global Goals, are a set of 17 interconnected goals established by the United Nations in 2015 to address various global challenges and promote sustainable development. These goals aim to achieve a more equitable, just, and sustainable world by 2030. The SDGs cover a wide range of social, economic, and environmental issues, and they provide a comprehensive framework for countries, organizations, and individuals to work together towards a more sustainable future

  • Origin and History of SDG

    • The origin of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) can be traced back to the United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development, also known as Rio+20, held in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, in 2012. During this conference, world leaders recognized the need for a more comprehensive and ambitious global development agenda to build upon the progress made under the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) and address emerging challenges.
    • The SDGs were formally adopted in September 2015 at the United Nations Sustainable Development Summit. This summit marked a historic moment when 193 UN member states came together to commit to the 17 SDGs and their associated 169 targets. The goals cover a wide range of issues, including poverty, hunger, health, education, gender equality, clean water, climate action, and more.The timeline for achieving the SDGs is set for 2030, providing a 15-year timeframe for countries and stakeholders to work towards achieving these goals. The SDGs have since become a central framework for international development efforts, guiding policies, initiatives, and actions worldwide, with a strong emphasis on leaving no one behind and creating a more sustainable and just future for all.

  • The 17 SDG's Are :

    1. No Poverty
    2. Zero Hunger
    3. Good Health and Well-being
    4. Quality Education
    5. Gender Equality
    6. Clean Water and Sanitation
    7. Affordable and Clean Energy
    8. Decent Work and Economic Growth
    9. Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure
    10. Reduced Inequality
    11. Sustainable Cities and Communities
    12. Responsible Consumption and Production
    13. Climate Action
    14. Life Below Water
    15. Life on Land
    16. Peace and Justice Strong Institutions
    17. Partnerships to achieve the Goal
